Welcome to

Wolver Hill

Asset Management Asia Limited

Wolver Hill Asset Management Asia Limited (“WHAMAL”) is part of the Wolver Hill group – an Asia-focused alternative investment asset management and advisory group established by Edward J. Rogers in 2006. In cooperation with its Tokyo-based affiliate Rogers Investment Advisors, WHAMAL provides best-in-class hedge fund platform, consulting and advisory services for alternative investment managers.

Our Products and Services

Fund Establishment

The Wolver Hill platform delivers innovative and cost-efficient solutions for emerging managers in Asia by providing full support for setting up new hedge funds businesses including the selection of top tier key service providers (lawyers, prime brokers, administrators, directors or trustees), coordination between all relevant parties, managing completion of fund documents, and the registration of all relevant vehicles. Given our team’s expertise and track record, the launch time of a new fund can be shortened to 2-4 months at a fraction of the cost.

Operations

Managers have access to our regulatory coverage either in Hong Kong through WHAMAL (licensed by the Hong Kong Securities and Futures Commission) and / or Tokyo through Rogers Investment Advisors K.K. (registered with the Kanto Finance Bureau as a registered investment advisor). Our team provides on-going operational support for hedge funds through Post-Launch Services. These services are supported and facilitated by a highly experienced team, state-of-the-art technology for day-to-day middle- and back-office operational support, outsourced COO-level support, as well as Mandarin, Cantonese and Japanese speakers on staff.

Infrastructure

Wolver Hill provides a top-tier supporting infrastructure including control, compliance and technology thereby allowing managers to focus on executing their strategies. WHAMAL provides fully furnished and equipped offices in central locations for immediate use by fund managers in Hong Kong and Tokyo. WHAMAL has invested in institutional-standard IT infrastructure required from a boutique hedge fund management company, including a state-of-the-art cloud-based Portfolio Management System – Beauchamp. All users in our office are supported by Principle One, an experienced IT consultant, well-known in the hedge fund industry in Hong Kong.

Offering innovative Asian investment solutions since 2014

Why Wolver Hill

Local Expertise

The firm, which is located in Tokyo and Hong Kong, is able to capitalize on local knowledge and networks to have a critical understanding, and to source the key relationships for hedge funds including all of the major Prime Brokers, Administrators, legal firms and software providers. Additionally, the team includes speakers of Mandarin, Cantonese, Japanese and English.

Manager Focused

Fund managers can choose to be based in either Hong Kong or Tokyo, and managers are not restricted to use any particular service providers, including fund administrators, execution systems or 3rd party marketers. Also, managers’ proprietary research remains their intellectual property, so we do not use their data for our proprietary trading.

Qualified Team

Operational issues that can greatly impact hedge fund manager returns behind the scenes can be addressed by our seasoned team of industry professionals based in Asia. The team uniquely combines decades of buy-side, sell-side, and service provider experience in Hedge Funds, Prime Brokerage and Asset Management. The Wolver Hill team offers high quality service.

Ed Rogers

Ed was previously Deutsche Bank Tokyo’s head of Prime Services sales and a member of DB Tokyo’s Equities Management committee. Prior to Deutsche Bank, Ed was a proprietary trader at Merrill Lynch in London, specializing in yield enhancement and corporate action arbitrage trades. Ed received a BA in History from Princeton University in 1987, and an MBA from Georgetown University in 1999.

Ken Chuen

Ken joined WHAMAL in 2015, and he oversees all business functions of the Hong Kong operations. Ken was previously the Chief Operating Officer at Matchpoint Investment Management Group where he oversaw all the business functions including the fund and Group’s operations, finance, human resources, technology, investor relations and compliance. Prior to joining Matchpoint, Ken worked at Deephaven Capital where he led the middle- office operations and trading support for their Hong Kong office. Prior to that, Ken held a similar role at Gandhara Advisors Asia. From 2005 until 2007, Ken worked at UBS Investment Bank where he had overall responsibility for the middle office operations of the cash equity proprietary desk, and for monitoring operational risks within the team. Ken started his career with PricewaterhouseCoopers as a tax associate with a focus on US and HK taxation. Ken holds a double degree in Commerce & Economics from Monash University, Melbourne and the designation of Chartered Alternative Investment Analyst (CAIA).

John Inamine

John oversees risk across the firm’s business lines. Most recently, Managing Director, Risk Management, at Nezu Asia, John was previously at SPARX Asset Management where he built up the fund of funds business from $50 million in AUM to $800 million, and later served as Head of Alternative Investments in Tokyo. Prior to that, John traded equity derivatives, beginning at O’Connor and Associates in Chicago, then at SBC Warburg in Tokyo where he ran the Japanese convertibles bonds book, and later at Deutsche Bank where he headed the Japanese convertible bonds trading and sales team. John also worked as an auditor for Arthur Young and Company after graduating from The Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania and prior to obtaining an MBA from The Haas School of Business at the University of California, Berkeley.

Sandeep Mand

Sandeep joined WHAMAL in 2017 to focus on business development and bring risk oversight experience to the group. He has close to 28yrs experience in the Fixed Income markets having spent 22yrs in Japan before moving to Hong Kong as Head of Rates Sales & Trading Asia (ex. Japan) for Mitsubishi UFJ Securities where he was responsible for building out their nascent rates platform. Prior to this he was a Managing Director at Societe Generale in Tokyo in a similar capacity. His experience covers not only the front office sales and trading functions but also the legal, regulatory, compliance, finance, IT, and operational aspects of running a cross-border Asian business. Sandeep holds a BEng in Avionics from Queen Mary College, London and a MSc. in Management Science from Imperial College, London.

Len Umantz, PhD, CAIA

Len was most recently an Executive Director at Morgan Stanley New York’s Securitized Products Group where he worked on relative value and capital structure arbitrage strategies in credit and mortgage derivatives and oversaw development of quantitative models for mortgage-backed securities trading. Prior to that, Mr. Umantz worked in fixed income and foreign exchange derivatives trading at Susquehanna International Group in London, where he was a part of a global exotics / hybrids trading desk covering Europe, Japan, and Asia. Len holds a PhD in Finance from Stanford University and an MS in Mathematics from Northwestern. During his doctoral studies he worked for Goldman Sachs Asset Management in New York and Bill Sharpe’s Financial Engines in Palo Alto.

Takio Sato

Prior to joining Rogers investment Advisors, Takio was responsible for relationship management of the Alternative Investment Services business for the entire APAC region at the Bank of New York Mellon. After joining BNYM in Tokyo in 2012, he later moved to their Hong Kong office being based there until April 2019. Prior to that, from 2006 to 2012, he was a Director / General Manager based in Singapore for United Managers Japan, a multi manager hedge fund platform, and he was responsible for all daily operations as the investment manager of the various funds on the platform. He started his career at Daiwa Securities in Tokyo and was later transferred to their Singapore office to run their listed derivatives desk as well as working at ABN AMRO and Credit Suisse focusing on providing various derivatives products to hedge funds. Takio majored in International Politics and Economics and holds a BA in Economics from Aoyama Gakuin University.
